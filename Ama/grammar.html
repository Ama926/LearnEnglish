TENSES

  • The concept of tense in English is a method that we use to refer to time - past, present and future.
  • Many languages use tense to talk about time.
  • We cannot talk of tenses without considering two components of many English tenses: time
    • Past - before now
    • Present - now, or any time that includes now
    • future - after now
    and aspect
    • progressive - uncompleted action
    • perfective - completed action or state

Simple Present Tense with Full Verbs

Uses:
  • To talk of things that happen repeatedly
  • Things you do as habits
  • Something that is true in general

formula

Plural Subject + Verb singular Subject + Verb + [s]
The student goes to school The students go to school



Simple Present Tense with BE Verbs

  • Verbs denote action.
  • But in English, all verbs do not mean action.
  • Sometimes we write sentences without action verbs
  • Then "BE" verbs helps to make sentences
With a noun With an adjective with "it" with "there"
My mother is a teacher The children are good It is windy today There are 20 girls in the class
