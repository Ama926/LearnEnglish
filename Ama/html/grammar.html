TENSES

  • The concept of tense in English is a method that we use to refer to time - past, present and future.
  • Many languages use tense to talk about time.
  • We cannot talk of tenses without considering two components of many English tenses: time
    • Past - before now
    • Present - now, or any time that includes now
    • future - after now
    and aspect
    • progressive - uncompleted action
    • perfective - completed action or state

Simple tenses


Simple Present Tense

1.With Full Verbs
2.With BE Verbs

Simple Present Tense with Full Verbs

Uses:
  • To talk of things that happen repeatedly
  • Things you do as habits
  • Something that is true in general

formula

Plural Subject + Verb singular Subject + Verb + [s]
The student goes to school The students go to school



Simple Present Tense with BE Verbs

  • Verbs denote action.
  • But in English, all verbs do not mean action.
  • Sometimes we write sentences without action verbs
  • Then "BE" verbs helps to make sentences
With a noun With an adjective with "it" with "there"
My mother is a teacher The children are good It is windy today There are 20 girls in the class



Simple Past Tense

1.With Full Verbs
2.With BE Verbs

Simple Past Tense with Full Verbs

Uses:
  • To refer things that happened or existed at a particular time in the past
  • We use the past tense forms of the Full Verbs
  • Something that is true in general

Full Verbs
Regular Irregular
Formula
V + ed V + ___
ex: kill + ed ex: take
Hunter Killed a lion He took a book



Simple Past Tense with BE Verbs

  • We use 'was' and 'were' of the verb BE in questions and answers to refer to things that existed in the past.
Singular : was Plural : were
I was at home yesterday My parents were good students



Simple Future Tense

  • We often need to talk about our future plans or what will happpens in the future.
  • Then we must talk use future tense
will + V[infinitive] BE + going to + V[infinitive]
We will have a concert at the end of this year
I will come to class tomorrow
I will go out tomorrow		 we are going to have a concert at the end of this year
It is going to rain today
I am going to enjoy this weekend



Summary of simple tenses...

Simple Past tense Simple Present tense Simple Future tense
subject + past verb + object subject + present verb + object subject + will + present verb(plural) + object
The teacher praised the children The teacher praises the children The teacher will praise the children

Continuous tenses


Present continuos tense

  • To describe planned work and live action
Singular Plural

Past Continuous Tense

  • use to describe something that was happening at a specif time in the past
Singular Plural
S + BE + V + ing S + BE + V + ing
I was talking to my friend The dogs were barking at that time



Future Continuous Tense

  • use to describe an incomplete action in progress in the future
will + be + V + ing I will be finished my degree next year by this time



Summary of continuous tenses...

Past continuous tense Present continuous tense Future continuous tense
subject + is/am/are + present participal + object subject + was/were + present participal + object subject + will + be + present participal + object
